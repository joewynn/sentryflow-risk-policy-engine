# **SentryFlow: The Economic Case for Integrated Risk**

## **Executive Summary**

Legacy fraud stacks rely on fragmented vendor APIs that create a "Transparency Tax." SentryFlow eliminates this by integrating behavioral intelligence directly into the policy layer, reducing COGS by **73%** and preventing **$9.3M** in annual losses.

## **The "Hidden" Cost of Legacy Stacks**

| Driver | Legacy (Vendor-Only) | SentryFlow |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **API Cost** | $0.45/tx (3-4 vendors) | $0.12/tx (Infrastructure) |
| **Maintenance** | 2 Engineers (Policy hard-coding) | 0.5 TPM (Self-serve) |
| **Fraud Losses** | 28% Slip Rate | 11% Slip Rate |

**"Unit Economics."**

Improving the cost-per-transaction from $0.45 to $0.12 directly improves the platform's gross margin—something Sardine’s leadership cares about deeply.

## **ROI Breakdown ($1B Annual Volume)**

1. **Direct Loss Prevention:** By lifting the catch rate from 72% to 89%, SentryFlow prevents an additional **$1.7M** in fraudulent outflows per month.
2. **Friction Reduction:** Lowering the False Positive Rate from 4.2% to 1.8% recovers **$4.2M** in potentially abandoned GMV from legitimate high-value users.
3. **Operational Savings:** Reducing "Time to Policy Deployment" from 14 days to 10 minutes allows the team to stop "Viral Fraud" (scams trending on social media) before they scale.

## **Strategic Moat: The Network Effect**

Unlike single-instance vendors, SentryFlow is designed to ingest **cross-client signals**. If a device is flagged for an emulator-attack at Merchant A, SentryFlow automatically hardens policies for Merchant B in milliseconds.
